# Interview Script — Product Owner

**Persona:** Product manager or product owner accountable for feature delivery and quality outcomes
**Duration:** 30 minutes
**Brief sections informed:** 3c, 3d, 4a, 5, 6-R

---

## 1. Warm-up & Context (3 min)

1. What product or surface area do you own?
2. What's your current relationship with the QA team — embedded, consultative, gate-only?

---

## 2. Feature Delivery Impact (8 min)

3. Of the features you've shipped this past quarter, how many were delayed and what was the cause? `[3c] [3d]`
4. How often is QA cited as the cause of delay? When that happens, in your assessment, is it accurate? `[3c]`
5. What's the typical lag between dev-complete and customer-available, and how much of that is QA? `[3d]`
6. Have you had to descope or defer features specifically because QA capacity ran out? `[3c] [3d]`

---

## 3. Quality Outcomes (8 min)

7. Think about the most painful customer-facing defects in the last 6 months. What pattern do they share? `[3c]`
8. Where do you feel today's QA process catches the wrong things, misses the right things, or both? `[3c]`
9. Of the recent customer escalations you can recall, can you walk me through 2–3 examples and where they came from — design gap, missed test, environmental, or something else? Don't try to estimate a percentage; specific examples are more useful than a number you'd be guessing at. `[3c] [3d]`
10. How do you currently weigh quality vs. speed when scoping a release? `[3c]`

---

## 4. Visibility & Communication (5 min)

11. What QA signal do you currently see, and is it timely enough to act on? `[3a] [3c]`
12. When QA finds a major issue late, how does that get communicated to you, and what's typically your response? `[3a] [3c]`
13. What information about test coverage or risk would change how you plan a release? `[5]`

---

## 5. Aspirational Future State (5 min)

14. If QA were no longer a delivery constraint, what would change about how you plan and ship? `[4a] [5]`
15. What outcome — measurable, business-visible — would convince you this initiative was worth the investment? `[5]`
16. If a new tool produced confident-but-wrong quality signals — false-pass or false-fail — how would that surface to your team, and what's the cost to you? `[6-R]`

---

## 6. Wrap-up (1 min)

17. What did I not ask about that I should have?
18. From a product perspective, what would make the strongest argument to leadership for funding this? `[1] [8]`
